When I think about Connecticut's future, I picture a state where families and communities are thriving because our state has chosen to invest in people, not incarceration. In that Connecticut, everyone, from every background, is treated fairly by the justice system. Do you want that world, too?

Connecticut has made progress toward ending mass incarceration, but we still have work to do. Every day, incarceration hurts families, communities, and economies in our state: Connecticut imprisons 334 percent more people than it did in 1968, an increase that far outpaces population growth and crime. Fair treatment in the justice system still depends on how much money you have and the color of your skin—racial disparities in Connecticut's prisons and jails are among the worst in the country.

We're about to turbocharge the fight to end mass incarceration in Connecticut. Are you with us?

Enter ACLU Smart Justice Connecticut, a new movement to cut Connecticut's prison and jail population by 50 percent and end racial disparities in the state's justice system. ACLU Smart Justice Connecticut is led by a strong cohort of formerly incarcerated people who are speaking truth to power, sharing our expertise, and building connections across the state.

Together, we'll be in the streets, at the legislature, and in the courts to end mass incarceration in Connecticut.

Will you sign the petition to be a Smart Justice supporter?

A Smart Justice supporter is anyone who wants Connecticut to cut its prison and jail population and end racial disparities in our state's justice system. By signing this petition, you'll show that ACLU Smart Justice Connecticut isn't alone in the quest for a free, fair, and just Connecticut—we've got you standing with us.
