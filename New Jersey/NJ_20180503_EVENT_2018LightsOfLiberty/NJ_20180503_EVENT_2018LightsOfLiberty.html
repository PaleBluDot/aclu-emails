Dear ##first_name[Supporter]##,

Even when things around the country can be bleak, we at the ACLU still have a lot of reasons for hope. I'm delighted to invite you to meet and honor some of the incredible community partners and clients who keep us inspired on Wednesday, May 9 at our Lights of Liberty Awards Dinner.

Some recent good news for our liberties:

  • The governor signed the strongest automatic voter registration law in the country and I had the honor of introducing him at the bill signing, while laying out our vision for improving democracy in New Jersey. We've also attended signing ceremonies for laws giving New Jerseyans earned sick leave and pay equity.

  • The New Jersey Supreme Court made it clear that public dollars cannot go to church repairs or construction. A ruling in another case means advocates will now get a chance to show why taxpayer funding for a yeshiva and seminary would unconstitutionally support religious education and training.

  • Our client who was given a jail sentence for not being able to immediately pay a littering ticket was vindicated with a court ruling that likened his punishment to extortion.

There's so much other important work that's ongoing: our representation of Indonesian Christian residents of Central Jersey at risk of deportation, our efforts to stand up for students who walked out after Parkland, and our role in leading the charge toward legalizing marijuana in the Garden State.

These are just a few of the things that keep us inspired. We couldn't do any of it without you, our supporters. We'd love to see you at Lights of Liberty to celebrate the reasons for optimism all around us. RSVP Today!

Amol Sinha
Executive Director
ACLU of New Jersey
