After a year of bail reform, the results are in – the near-elimination of money bail in New Jersey has led to greater justice and public safety. And the entire country is paying attention.

We are excited to take part in a panel discussion sponsored by The Marshall Project and WNYC radio to reflect on that first year – Bail Reform: One Year Later, on Thursday, February 22.

Bail Reform: One Year Later

Some of the leading thinkers on criminal justice will take part, including our Senior Supervising Attorney Alexander Shalom, who was instrumental in bringing about bail reform and is still working to make it as strong as possible.

WNYC's Nancy Solomon will moderate the discussion, featuring – in addition to Alex – the administrative director of the New Jersey courts, the director of the New Jersey division on criminal justice, the New Jersey public defender, and the second vice president of the state bar association.

The future of criminal justice reform will be decided in part by what happens in New Jersey. The people leading the way will be on one stage, talking about that work. You really don't want to miss this.
