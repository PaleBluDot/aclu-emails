May 3, 2019 was sine die of the legislative session. Sine die is Latin for the phrase "without assigning another meeting” and it's the term people under the dome use to describe the last day of the 120-day session.

We plotted an ambitious legislative agenda and with 14 proactive bills in our pocket, 13 passed, 3 have been signed by the Governor and another 10 await his signature.

Our legislative agenda focused on pre-trial reform and bringing relief to our immigrant neighbors. Also, consistent with the belief that our schools should be a place of inclusion, we championed comprehensive sex education and it will be funded by the State for the first time ever.

Visit our website for a full list of our 2019 legislative victories.

We would like to thank the thousands of ACLU activists statewide who made this year's legislative session an unparalleled success. You made hundreds of calls, sent hundreds of texts, signed hundreds of postcards and attended town halls. You gave true meaning to — and indeed brought to life the phrase — "if you want to go fast, go alone. But if you want to go far, go together.” Together, we went far. Take a bow!

Thanks for sticking with us,

Denise Maes
Public Policy Director, ACLU of Colorado
