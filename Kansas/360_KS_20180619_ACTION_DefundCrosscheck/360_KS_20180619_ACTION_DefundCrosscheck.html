Crosscheck is a threat to privacy and places thousands at risk of identity theft. The suit, Moore v. Kobach, was brought because Secretary Kobach's actions and operation of the Crosscheck program violated the plaintiffs' 14th Amendment right to informational privacy. In addition, Secretary Kobach's actions were egregious violations of the Kansas Public Records Act, which prohibits the disclosure of any document containing a Social Security number when that document also contains an individual's personal information, and impacted Republican, Democratic, and Unaffiliated voters alike — leaving them vulnerable to identity theft.

We bring the Moore suit right on the heels of a decision in another, earlier suit, Fish v. Kobach. On June 18, a federal court sided with our challenge of Kansas' documentary proof-of-citizenship law, saying it violated the National Voter Registration Act and the Constitution. During our three-week trial in March, we showed that Kobach's voter restrictions disenfranchised thousands of voters and were based in xenophobic myths of noncitizens engaging in rampant election fraud.
