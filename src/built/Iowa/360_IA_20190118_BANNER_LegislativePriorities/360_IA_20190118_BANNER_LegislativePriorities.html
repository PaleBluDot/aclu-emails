The Iowa Legislature is underway, and we’re ready. Each session, it's a tough task to monitor the dozens if not hundreds of bills that could impact civil liberties in our state. But this year we're fully staffed with multiple people who will be at the statehouse, including Policy Director Daniel Zeno, contract lobbyist Pete McRoberts and Executive Director Mark Stringer.

We'll be focusing our energies on:

  • Pregnant Workers Fairness Act. Most Iowa businesses offer reasonable accommodations to pregnant workers, but some still do not. That needs to change. Find out more.
  • Criminal justice reform. Mass incarceration in Iowa has grown dramatically in the last 40 years. Iowa lawmakers across the political spectrum are realizing that it's time to rethink why and how we incarcerate people in our state. Read more.
  • Restoring voting rights for Iowans with felony convictions. With Florida rescinding its law for most people last November, Iowa and Kentucky are now the only states that automatically and permanently take away the right to vote for people with a felony conviction. We're glad Gov. Reynolds has voiced her support for this and we'll continue our work to get this changed.
  • Racial Profiling. Racial profiling feeds racial disparities in our criminal justice system. We'll once again work with partner organizations, including the NAACP, for meaningful legislation that makes it more difficult for law enforcement to wrongly target people of color. Find out more.

Thanks for sticking with us,

ACLU of Iowa
