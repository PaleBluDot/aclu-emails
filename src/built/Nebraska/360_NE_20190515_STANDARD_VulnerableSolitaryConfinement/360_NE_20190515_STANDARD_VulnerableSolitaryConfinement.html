The Nebraska Legislature is considering a bill containing several meaningful smart justice and prison reform measures this afternoon. One of the most important parts of this bill is an end to solitary confinement for the most vulnerable incarcerated Nebraskans.

Senator Steve Lathrop and Senator Tony Vargas are demonstrating strong and compassionate leadership to end solitary confinement for those who are pregnant, developmentally disabled, and living with severe mental illness. Nebraska remains an outlier in the overuse of this suspect practice despite the fact that there is a clear consensus from the medical community that solitary confinement is a modern-day human rights abuse for the mentally ill.

Vulnerable Nebraskans live in conditions of confinement that include extreme isolation with no or very limited human contact, no or very limited fresh air or exercise, and without meaningful mental healthcare. This is one small but very important policy that is desperately needed to reform Nebraska’s crisis-riddled prison system. This is about human rights, disability rights, racial justice, and mass incarceration. Senators need to hear from the voters in their district today.

Please take action today – tell the Nebraska Legislature to support LB686 and end solitary for vulnerable Nebraskans.

Thanks for standing with us,

Danielle Conrad
Executive Director, ACLU of Nebraska
