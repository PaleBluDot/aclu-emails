This year, we are also grateful for the volunteers who joined text and phonebanks to get out the vote and for the SoCal voters who turned out at record levels for down-ballot measures. Learn more about our electoral work.

Two statewide initiatives – Propositions 17 and 20 – and Los Angeles County's Measure J deserve recognition for the ways that they will bring California a step closer towards true justice. Here's why:

  • Prop 17 expands our democracy. It amends the California Constitution to allow people on parole the right to vote. This means that some 50,000 people returning to their communities can now cast a ballot for the policies and officials that will impact their lives.
  • Prop 20 tried to erode critical justice reforms – and failed. It would have rolled back the reforms that Californians had already approved at the ballot box, which are aimed at decreasing the population of our state's sprawling prison system. As such, it would have deepened the racial disparities that lie at the heart of the incarceration system. Californians soundly rejected it.
  • Measure J invests in services and resources L.A. County truly needs. The measure permanently allocates at least 10 percent of locally-controlled revenues for direct community investments in community health, housing and jobs, and alternatives to incarceration. This historic win – led by Black and Latinx directly-impacted leaders and families – was built on a decade of work to move the county to a care-first, jails-last approach.
