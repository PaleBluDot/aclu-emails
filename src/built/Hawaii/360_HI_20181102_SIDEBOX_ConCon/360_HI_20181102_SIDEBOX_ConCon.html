We're urging you to vote with everyone’s civil rights in mind on November 6 and join us in opposing a constitutional convention.

Around the country, we see government and special interest groups dismantling labor rights, invading our privacy, rolling back hard-won LGBTQ protections, and advancing private ownership of once-public spaces, just to name a few. Those same systems of power would like nothing more than a crack at our state constitution.

Hawaii's constitution provides more civil rights protections than the U.S. Constitution and a constitutional convention would open the entire document up for revision — a risk we cannot afford to take, especially in these turbulent and uncertain times.

While the U.S. Constitution can and does set one floor for our civil rights — below which no state may go — individual state constitutions can set a different, higher floor. The Hawai'i constitution does just that. Just a few examples include an explicit right to privacy, equality of rights regardless of sex (the equivalent of the Equal Rights Amendment, which still has not been ratified as part of the U.S. Constitution), workers' rights to organize, the right not to be imprisoned because of debt, the right to keep your partisan affiliation private, protections against segregation in the military, and more.

Hawaii's constitution is one of the most progressive in the nation and it needs our care and protection. Once a constitutional convention is convened, there's really no limit to the types of changes that can be made. We have a lot to lose by cracking it wide open — and by voting NO on November 6 we are standing up to defend it and protect the civil rights and liberties it affords us all.

Learn more about what a constitution convention would mean for civil rights and liberties in Hawai’i in our opinion piece that ran on Tuesday, October 30 in the Honolulu Civil Beat.
