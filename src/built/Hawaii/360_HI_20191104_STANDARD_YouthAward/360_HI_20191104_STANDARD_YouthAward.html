Do you know a local student who has an innovative project idea that would enhance equality for the LGBTQ+ community but needs a little help getting it off the ground? Send them our way! Are you such a student yourself? Read on!

The ACLU of Hawai'i is excited to announce the inaugural launch of a student scholarship program that will provide financial support to two local students with innovative project ideas aimed at furthering the civil rights of LGBTQ+ people in Hawai'i.

Made possible by the Tom Humphreys and Allan Wang Civil Rights Fund, the fund will grant two cash gifts – each for $3,900 – one to a high school student aged 16 to 18, and one to a college or graduate school student aged 19 to 24. It is meant to be used toward a proposed project categorized into one of four categories: advocacy, arts, education/journalism, or technology.

This fund was established on November 8, 2011 by Allan Wang in memory of his late husband Tom Humphreys, scientist, businessman and professor at the University of Hawai'i at MƒÅnoa. With Wang by his side, Humphreys was a proud board member and supporter of the ACLU of Hawai'i and steadfast activist for civil rights here in Hawai'i. He played a pivotal role in the legalization of same-sex marriage, which occurred the year before his death.

The application deadline for this grant is Wednesday, November 20, 2019. Awardees will be notified in December 2019 and announced shortly thereafter.

Visit our website to learn more about the scholarship and how to apply.

We look forward to reading your applications! Mahalo,
