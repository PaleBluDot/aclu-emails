Dear ##SM_RULE_360 Email Salutation##,

Marijuana reform is about racial justice. Law enforcement uses marijuana all the time as an excuse to stop and search Virginians, especially Black Virginians. Charlottesville recently released data showing that Black people there are five times more likely than white people to be subjected to "stop-and-frisk" practices.

Decriminalizing simple marijuana possession still lets police keep the option of stopping people for perceived marijuana violations, even if the punishment for that violation is a civil fine rather than a criminal conviction. We know law enforcement uses marijuana as a racist tool to target Black people, so why would we let them keep that tool?

Lawmakers should just repeal the prohibition on simple marijuana possession. They can do that right now by supporting House Bill 1507 from Del. Jennifer Carroll Foy. Let your lawmakers know that decriminalization isn't the answer.
