Have you read about our latest report? We recently released Women in the Criminal Justice System: Pathways to Incarceration to highlight the impact of mass incarceration on women in Virginia, the ways women become entangled in the criminal justice system, and the best path forward to reduce incarceration and treat underlying issues. The release of this report also launched an advocacy campaign to put public pressure on stakeholders and lawmakers to make reforms for women in the criminal justice system.

  • #WomenInPrison: Kemba Smith, a formerly incarcerated woman, advocate, and author of "Poster Child: The Kemba Smith Story," believes that we can't end mass incarceration without changing our mindset about women's involvement with crimes. She shared her experiences with us and why she supports the ACLU of Virginia's new report on Women in the Criminal Justice System.

  • On the Docket: We filed a lawsuit against the Virginia Department of Corrections on behalf of our client, Nicolas Reyes, who has been held in solitary confinement for more than 12 years. From Vishal Agraharkar, our Senior Staff Attorney: "We filed this suit to bring an end to these discriminatory and cruel practices for Mr. Reyes, and to shine a light on the wide gulf between the Department of Corrections' rhetoric and its actual practices." Read more about Mr. Reyes and the widespread use of solitary confinement in Virginia.

  • Save the Date: Banned Books Week is September 23-29, and we're working with Chop Suey in Richmond and other independent bookstores across the commonwealth to celebrate the First Amendment and speak out against censorship. Follow us on Facebook to stay up to date on those events.

    Do you live in Chesterfield County? We're hosting a community forum on Monday, October 23 with John Childrey and Scott Miles, the candidates for Chesterfield County Commonwealth's Attorney. The new CA will be elected November 6 and will have the unparalleled authority to decide who gets released on bail, who gets a plea deal, and which cases go to trial. Before either candidate is given that much power over the citizens of Chesterfield County, we have some questions.

  • ICYMI: ACLU of Virginia members, community advocates and allies came together last weekend at the ACLU of Virginia's 50th Anniversary Kick-Off and Annual Meeting to highlight women in the criminal justice system. Read about the event from our paralegal, Marcie Hampton, and take a look at the photos on our Facebook page.
