Dear ##SM_RULE_360 Email Salutation##,

Robert E. Lee surrendered the Confederate Army 154 years ago today at the Appomattox Court House, yet his imposing likeness still stands in the middle of Monument Avenue in Richmond. It was built as a Jim Crow-era symbol of racism and oppression, and the governor has the ability under state law to take it down and relocate it to a place where it can be viewed in historical context.

The Virginia Department of General Services is considering making regulations permanent that were enacted on an "emergency" basis in 2019 regarding how the state will permit protests and demonstrations at the Lee Monument. The answer to the problem of how to balance public safety and the right to free expression at this public forum is not to enact burdensome, illegal regulations. No regulations will be needed at all if Gov. Ralph Northam uses his executive authority to remove the statue from Monument Avenue.

The Lee Monument is a state-property island in the middle of Richmond, and if Gov. Northam is committed to racial equity, he should immediately remove this towering racist symbol.

Tell Gov. Northam today, on the anniversary of Lee’s surrender, that the Lee Monument belongs in a more appropriate context for those who want to see it.

Very truly yours,

Claire G. Gastanaga
Executive Director, ACLU of Virginia
