Dear ##SM_RULE_360 Email Salutation##,

19 years ago, Massachusetts blocked incarcerated people in state prison from voting in statewide elections. The next year, the state went further, barring access to presidential and municipal elections. Our state's history of disenfranchisement is shameful, and it's time to do something about it.

The ACLU of Massachusetts is proud to support Mass POWER in their effort to restore voting rights. Sign-up to gather signatures for a ballot initiative that would let incarcerated people reclaim their voice.

Disenfranchisement has long been one of the most insidious aspects of our criminal legal system, and a cornerstone of racism in our state and federal government. By denying the vote to incarcerated Americans – disproportionately people of color – we perpetuate systems of oppression for future generations.

It's time for Massachusetts to live up to its democratic principles. It's time to join our neighbors in Vermont and Maine and restore the right to vote for people who are incarcerated.

We hope you'll help us spread the word.

In solidarity,

ACLU of Massachusetts
