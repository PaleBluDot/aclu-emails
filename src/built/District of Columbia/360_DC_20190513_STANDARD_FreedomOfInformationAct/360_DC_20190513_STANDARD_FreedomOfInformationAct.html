The D.C. Council is about to gut a transparency law in the least transparent way possible.

Dear ##SM_RULE_360 Email Salutation##,

The D.C. Council will be voting Tuesday, May 14, on a stealth amendment to the D.C. Freedom of Information Act.

Attached to a budget report less than two weeks ago, with no public notice or hearing, the amendments would gut the law by requiring those who make Freedom of Information requests to describe the documents they want "with particularity."

But that's impossible — people don't know the details of documents they haven't seen!

The amendments would also allow agencies to withhold records they think are not "related to the conduct of public business," thus covering up evidence of private wrongdoing by government officials. We saw one such instance earlier this year, when the Washington Post revealed Councilmember Jack Evans had used his official Council email address to solicit business for his private consulting company. Those emails were obtained through a Freedom of Information Act request.

These proposals are bad enough, but to enact them with no opportunity for public input, and to do it in a budget bill when they have nothing to do with the budget, is unacceptable.

Contact your Councilmembers today and tell them to remove the FOIA amendments from the budget bill.

Thank you for your quick action!

Nassim Moshiree
Policy Director, ACLU of the District of Columbia
