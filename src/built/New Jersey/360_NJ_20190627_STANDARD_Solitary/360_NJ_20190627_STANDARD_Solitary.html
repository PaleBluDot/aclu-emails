Dear ##SM_RULE_360 Email Salutation##,

We're so close. In late June, both chambers of the state Legislature passed a bill to dramatically restrict the use of solitary confinement in New Jersey prisons and jails.

The solitary bill just needs Governor Phil Murphy's signature to become law and to make history. He has until early August to sign.

Email Gov. Murphy to tell him: we need you to sign the bill, and we need you to sign it as-is.

Gov. Murphy has not indicated whether he would sign the bill with no changes. We cannot allow thousands of New Jerseyans, many in vulnerable groups, to languish in isolation every day.

Take Action

The Star-Ledger ran a powerful editorial on Sunday, June 30:

"Being denied meaningful human contact for too long causes psychological agony. It's the way we're built. Solitary confinement breaks people, and causes some to do horrible things."

"The United Nations says isolation should be banned except in the most extreme circumstances. The question is, will Phil Murphy take its advice in New Jersey?"

"As many as 1,500 people are in isolation on any given day in New Jersey, Tess Borden, an attorney with the ACLU, estimates."

Similar legislation has passed before. But then Gov. Christie vetoed an earlier version of the bill in 2016. We need Gov. Murphy to undo Christie's mistake by signing this bill unchanged. A veto of this bill, including a conditional veto, would only be a continuation of Christie's legacy.

This bill would make legislative history by:

  • Imposing strict limits on the amount of time anyone can spend in solitary.
  • Banning solitary altogether for the most vulnerable people — people 21 and younger, 65 and older, people who have serious medical conditions or mental illness, pregnant people, and LGBTQ people, among others.
  • Requiring important mental health evaluations before and during any use of solitary confinement, requires data collection and reports, and applies not only to state prisons but also to county jails — including those that detain immigrants.

Urge Gov. Murphy to do the right thing. We've already waited too long.

Amol Sinha
Executive Director, ACLU of New Jersey

P.S. Once you've taken action, read the full editorial — and in particular, watch the powerful videos of survivors of solitary confinement telling their stories.
