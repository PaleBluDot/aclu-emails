Early in-person voting in Louisiana starts today – Friday, October 16 – and it's no exaggeration to say that our human rights, our civil liberties, and our democracy are all at stake.

This year justice is on the ballot, Black lives are on the ballot, reproductive freedom is on the ballot – and all of us have a role to play. The good news is more than 10 million voters have already cast their ballots, putting the country on track for record-shattering turnout in November.

Starting today, registered voters in Louisiana can go to an early voting polling place any time from 8 a.m. to 7 p.m. and cast their ballot. Don't delay! This early voting period ends on Tuesday, October 27. Visit Secretary of State's website to find an early voting polling location near you.

With such high stakes in this election, we must ensure all voters' voices are heard loud and clear. If you see or experience voter intimidation while at the polls, use our new Mobile Justice App to record it and send it directly to us at the ACLU. Please note: This link will take you to a third-party website, apple.com.

Here is a quick checklist to help you vote like your rights depend on it.

  1. Find your polling place on the Secretary of State's website.
  2. Research the issues and candidates, including down-ballot races like District Attorney.
  3. Save the Election Protection Hotline: 1-866-OUR-VOTE.
  4. Download the Mobile Justice App on the Apple App Store.
  5. Go to the polls, bringing your photo ID and face covering.

We can't let anyone silence us. Make sure your voice is heard by casting a ballot today!
