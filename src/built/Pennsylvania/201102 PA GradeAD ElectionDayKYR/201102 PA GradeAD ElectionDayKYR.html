This election season has not been an easy one. But the good news is, tomorrow is Election Day! Many of you have already cast your ballot by opting to vote by mail or vote early at a satellite election location. For those of you who haven't, it's okay – the polls will be open tomorrow from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. To help you navigate the day, below is an Election Day guide:

  1. Make sure you know your rights at the polls. Find all the information you need in our downloadable pdf.
  2. If you are transgender, non-binary, or a person worried about having a photo ID, know that only voters who are voting for the first time in their election district need to show ID. Acceptable ID includes both photo and non-photo ID. More information about ID can be found in the FAQ section of aclupa.org/vote.
  3. If you applied to vote by mail but never received your ballot, you can vote provisionally at the polls.
  4. If you have your mail-in ballot but made a mistake; received your ballot so late that you are worried about putting it in the mail; or changed your mind about voting by mail, you can vote at the polls. Bring your ballot and the return envelope to your polling place on Election Day. Poll workers will cancel it, and you can vote on a machine. (You must bring the entire mail-in ballot package, including the envelope with your name on it.)
  5. Double-check the location of your polling place. You can look it up here.
  6. Research what's on your ballot before you show up at the polls. You can go to Ballotpedia.org.
  7. Just in case the line at your polling place is long, pack water, food, and entertainment (a book, music, etc.). If the polls close while you're still in line, stay in line. You have the right to vote.
  8. If you have questions, witness, or experience voter intimidation, your polling place doesn't have the proper resources, or you need help with anything voting-related, we're here for you! You can call or text the nonpartisan election protection hotline that is staffed by experts from the Election Protection Coalition and trained volunteers. Voter guardians will also be present at some polling locations.
    • English: 866-OUR-VOTE (866-687-8683)
    • Español and English: 888-VE-Y-VOTA (888-839-8682)
    • (عربى Arabic and English: 844-YALLA-US (844-925-5287)
    • Mandarin (普通話), Cantonese (廣東話; 話), Korean (한국어), Vietnamese (Tiếng Việt), Tagalog, Urdu, Hindi, Bengali, and English: 888-API-VOTE (888-274-8683)
  9. Finally, wear a mask, use hand sanitizer, and stay safe.

For more voting information about your rights, visit aclupa.org/vote. This is the home stretch! We can do this.
