Fall is in the air, and school is once again in session. Do the students in your life know their rights? Whether it's drug testing, taking a knee, enrollment of immigrant students, or censorship (happy banned books week!), we'd like to help you learn.

Students and parents: if your rights are violated in schools, we want to hear from you, and we want to hear your stories.

Earlier this month, Randolph High School in Morris County became the latest to drug-test students under questionable circumstances. As many as 75 students were forced to take an alcohol test or risk suspension. Fewer than five students tested positive.

Here's what all students and parents need to know about drug testing in schools:

  • When substance use is based on suspicion, schools can only order drug testing if the suspicion is reasonable and individualized, not based on a hunch.
  • Schools can have a random drug-testing program for students who participate in extracurricular activities or park on campus if they have a special need for it.
  • Students have the right to protest as long as it doesnâ€™t cause a substantial disruption of school operations.
  • Books and student work can't be censored based on disagreement with a specific idea or message.
  • Every student, of any immigration status, has an equal right under the U.S. and New Jersey Constitutions to obtain a public education.
