At least 12 people have been killed in police-involved shootings this year in New Jersey alone. And unless the New Jersey Attorney General decides to step in, local county prosecutors, who work closely with local police, will be the ones handling these cases.

We need a vote on A1115 – a bill to require independent prosecutors to investigate killings by police instead of local law enforcement.

Our current system creates fertile ground for conflicts of interest. Local county prosecutors work day-in, day-out with local police, relying on mutual trust and cooperation to perform their jobs. A more independent body, like the Attorney General's office, should be the agency investigating such deaths, not local prosecutors.

Independent prosecutors handling investigations of killings by police is fundamental to holding police accountable. Momentum has been building around this issue nationally, and President Obama's Task Force on 21st Century Policing specifically recommended law enforcement take this step. New Jersey's Senate voted yes already. We need the Assembly to vote yes, too.

Demand accountability and tell Assembly Speaker Prieto to put A1115 up for a vote as soon as possible.
