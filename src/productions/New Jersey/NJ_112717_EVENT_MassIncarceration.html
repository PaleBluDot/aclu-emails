Mass incarceration is a major civil rights crisis of our time. About 35,000 people are incarcerated in New Jersey alone. 60 percent are Black, even though they represent only 15 percent of the state's population.

We're confronting this crisis as a community on Wednesday, November 29. We hope you'll join us.

Advocates and artists are holding a panel discussion and community press conference at Shine Portrait Studio to mark the opening of "The Missing," a haunting, profound new exhibition at the Aljira Center for Contemporary Art in Newark. "The Missing" explores the toll our system of mass incarceration has taken on our communities, especially people of color. RSVP to attend.

This event will feature a wide range of brilliant activists and artists. These creative leaders are changing our criminal justice system, our perspectives, and our wider world:

  • Duron Jackson, the artist who created "The Missing."

  • Portia Allen-Kyle and Tess Borden from the ACLU of New Jersey.

  • Dexter Wimberly of Aljira, a Center for Contemporary Art.

  • Ryan Haygood from the New Jersey Institute for Social Justice.

  • Artists from Shine Portrait Studio.

The ACLU panelists will preview and discuss a new report we're about to release – A Vision to End Mass Incarceration in New Jersey – describing an aspirational, broad-reaching path forward to drastically reduce mass incarceration in our state.

Mass incarceration systematically excludes people of color from housing, employment, and democracy itself. Yet there is hope – a growing number of people agree that this brutal system must end. We look forward to sharing our own vision for curtailing mass incarceration with you on November 29. RSVP today.

Sincerely,

Amol Sinha
Executive Director
ACLU of New Jersey
