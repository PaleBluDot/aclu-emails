Recently, Florida surpassed Texas as the state with the most banned books in the country. This effort to restrict free speech in the classroom is part of a broader movement by state legislators to limit rights across the board, including gender equality, LGBTQ+ equality, and voting rights. With over 40% of all book bans nationwide taking place in Florida school districts, several classrooms began the school year with empty shelves.

Here is a closer look at the state of censorship in Florida, according to Pen America's recent report:

  • More than 1,400 books have been banned throughout the state.
  • Of the books that have been banned, most bans target the experiences of LGBTQ+ and people of color.
  • Clay County School District is leading the state in book bans, with over 400 book objections.

It's important to understand that students have a right to see themselves reflected in their libraries and learn about issues from different perspectives. Florida communities play a vital role in these discussions. Engaging with parents, teachers, and community members in open dialogues can help address concerns while preserving intellectual freedom.

If you or someone you know has been impacted by educational censorship, we want to hear from you. Together, we can fight against censorship and ensure that everyone has the right to read freely.
