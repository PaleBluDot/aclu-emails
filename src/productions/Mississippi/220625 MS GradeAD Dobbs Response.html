Whether it was devastation or rage, we all felt a wave of emotion yesterday as the Supreme Court announced the overturning of Roe v. Wade.

Mississippi politicians are getting ready to turn back the clock nearly 50 years on our fundamental rights and force women and everyone who can become pregnant into a second-class status.

In less than two weeks, the state's only abortion clinic will be forced to close its doors as the abortion trigger ban law goes into effect. This will have a devastating impact on women and all people who can become pregnant, taking from them a right that has been central to their ability to plan their lives, families, and careers. But the burdens will disproportionately fall on Black women, those struggling to make ends meet, young people, rural residents, undocumented immigrants, and the LGBTQ+ communities.

As we went into a rapid response to the news, I also reflected. In the hallways of our office, I was reminded of why I chose to work at the ACLU of Mississippi. I gazed at sketches of James Chaney, Andrew Goodman, and Michael Schwerner and murals of Fannie Lou Hamer and Medgar Evers. The artwork reminds us that white supremacy and state-sponsored hate have caused terrible physical and mental pain in Mississippi. But in these times, the pieces also remind us that progress is possible. Our state and country have indeed come a long way.

I want to be clear; we are under attack, and the Supreme Court ruling will impact more than reproductive rights. The opinion also signals that the right to access birth control and gender-affirming care, marry who we love, and vote could soon be overturned. We are in the middle of another great redemption. There are people that would rather destroy this country than share it.

Anti-abortion politicians have put Mississippi on the wrong side of history for too long, and the ACLU of Mississippi is determined not to let them off the hook. Politicians who do not believe in protecting our civil rights and liberties have no business in governors' mansions, state attorneys general's offices, state supreme court benches, or state legislatures. We will be here to hold them to account.

Thank you for standing beside us while we work and face these obstacles.
