Our jails and prisons are over capacity and each year the Wyoming Legislature doubles down on the overtaxed system by creating new crimes and moving toward longer, harsher penalties for violating existing laws.

Tell your legislator that it's time to reduce Wyoming's prison population.

The "tough on crime" tactics of the past are ineffective and have resulted in a justice system that is overburdened and costly. Fortunately, the Wyoming Legislature has the power to reverse course and fix our broken criminal justice system. We released a report today recommending steps the legislature can take to start reducing Wyoming's growing prison population, including:

  • Probation and parole reform;

  • Establishing a boot camp program for women; and

  • Revisiting drug laws already on the books that are responsible for sending people to prison for crimes that could be dealt with in other areas of the justice system.

Read our report and then contact your legislators and tell them to take steps to reduce incarceration rates, keep our communities safe, and ensure our state is fiscally sound.
