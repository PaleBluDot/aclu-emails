No one should be forced to carry a pregnancy against their will or face the life-altering consequences of being denied essential health care. Yet, following the Supreme Court's overturning of Roe v. Wade, half of the states are anticipated to enact abortion bans in the coming months. Many have already done so.

In Vermont, we can fight back and show other states what it means to truly protect reproductive freedom by voting yes on Article 22, the Reproductive Liberty Amendment, in November.

In order to help our friends and neighbors better understand how important it is to be able to access reproductive health care, we're collecting stories from Vermonters about having an abortion, the impacts of being denied abortion services, difficulties accessing care, or working as an abortion activist or provider.

Share your story

With your permission, we will share some of these stories publicly, including on social media, to help build support for protecting reproductive freedom and passing the Reproductive Liberty Amendment at this critical time.

Article 22 is about more than abortion – it's about ensuring every person's freedom to make their own reproductive decisions, including whether and when to become pregnant or use temporary or permanent birth control. We urge you to share your stories with your friends and neighbors, and to vote like your rights depend on it this year – because they do.
