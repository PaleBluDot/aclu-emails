Today we are proud to release our latest comprehensive report, Women in the Criminal Justice System: Pathways to Incarceration in Virginia. Over the last three years, we spent hundreds of hours talking with women across the Commonwealth about their experiences in the criminal justice system, researching the issues that affect women in prisons and jails, and creating a set of recommendations to alleviate the inequality that women face within the criminal justice system.

In Virginia and across the country, women are being incarcerated at rates that are increasing much more rapidly than those for men. The over-incarceration of women is a symptom of a complex network of social barriers, economic inequality, reproductive injustice, and racial and sexual discrimination deeply woven into our society. Our report is intended to be the first step in a long campaign to reform the criminal justice system for all women in Virginia.

To kick off this reform campaign, we're hosting a press conference at 11 a.m. today, and we invite you to watch our livestream on Facebook.

During the press conference, you'll hear directly from formerly incarcerated women on their experiences in the criminal justice system and their challenges with re-entry. We'll review our recommendations to stakeholders who have the power to lower incarceration rates, address underlying issues causing over-incarceration, and ensure women's rights are not infringed upon when they encounter the criminal justice system.

This report is the beginning of a tough fight for equality and fairness for women across Virginia. We hope you'll join us, not just today, but on the long road ahead.
