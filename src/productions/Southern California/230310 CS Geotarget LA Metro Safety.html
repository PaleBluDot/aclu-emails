Imagine descending onto your closest Metro station: there's a bustling food court with all your favorites; corridors festooned with vibrant murals and poetry in action by local artists; clean platforms and passageways well-lit and well-staffed to help you get where you're going. Imagine taking a power nap or finally finishing that book in tow before you get to your destination.

Take action and sign the petition now to keep our Metro safe.

Next Thursday, March 16, the L.A.Metro Board of Directors – a public body made up of various city and county officials – will vote to invest another $222 million into policing on transit. That would be a move in the wrong direction.

We've said it over and over again: more policing does not make us safe. Officers stop and cite more Black transit riders than any other community, despite Black riders making up only 18 percent of Metro ridership. Officers also target poor and unhoused people accessing public services. We know there are more effective responses to commuter safety that respect people regardless of their race or income.

Last year, the board established a program of unarmed transit ambassadors who can address emergencies with professionalism and care. Right now, as it considers renewing contracts, the board should continue to invest in transit ambassadors and Metro infrastructure, like better lighting, more reliable timetables and service, and fareless transit.

I've never felt more connected to my community and my city than when I took a bus or train, and L.A. Metro has the resources to build stations that foster community and care-first approaches to safety.

Join me in demanding that the Metro Board discontinue contracts with law enforcement, reduce waste on redundant and harmful police patrol, and fund approaches that actually keep commuters safe.
