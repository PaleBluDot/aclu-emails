Ithaka is a documentary film that shines a light on what the most powerful nations will do to hide their crimes. With Assange facing a 175-year sentence if extradited to the U.S., his family members are confronting the prospect of losing him to the abyss of our criminal legal system. This David-and-Goliath struggle is personal – and with Assange's health declining in a British maximum-security prison, the clock is ticking.

Following the screening, ACLU SoCal Executive Director Hector Villagra will join Assange's father, John Shipton, and Assange's brother and producer of Ithaka, Gabriel Shipton, in a conversation moderated by ACLU SoCal Board President Rana Sharif. You don't want to miss this historic event.
