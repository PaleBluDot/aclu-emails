When your governor calls a bill "the most extreme anti-choice legislation in the country," you know you've got a problem.

Welcome to Pennsylvania, the state that has proposed some of the most aggressive and restrictive women's health and health care access bills in the country. While this isn't new, restrictions enacted in Pennsylvania in the 1980s were the subject of the landmark 1992 Supreme Court decision in Planned Parenthood of Southeastern Pennsylvania v. Casey, the state legislature has ramped up its attacks this legislative session.

Join us on Thursday, June 14 for our first ever all-member conference call. ACLU-PA Senior Policy Advocate Julie Zaebst and Legislative Director Elizabeth Randol will detail how the conversation about abortion rights has evolved in recent years and look at the nuances of the legislation that we and our allies have been fighting during the 2017-2018 legislative session.
