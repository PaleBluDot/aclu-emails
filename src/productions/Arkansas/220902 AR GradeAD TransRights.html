The 8th U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals affirmed that no child should be denied the medical care they need. The appellate court ruled, "there is substantial evidence to support the district court's conclusion that the Act prohibits medical treatment that conforms with the recognized standard of care." For transgender youth in Arkansas, this means they can still access life-saving gender-affirming health care.

This decision comes as we continue our fight against a 2021 law attempting to ban gender-affirming health care for transgender adolescents. Research shows that denying gender-affirming care to transgender youth contributes to depression, isolation, eating disorders, self-harm, and suicide. Our lawsuit challenging this ban begins trial in October, and we are relieved that as we prepare for trial, care will remain available.

Transgender people deserve the right to live healthy lives without fear and discrimination.
