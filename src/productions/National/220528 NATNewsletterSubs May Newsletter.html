ROE'S UNCERTAIN FUTURE

To See the Future of Roe, Look to the States

There are some things we don't know about the leaked Supreme Court opinion in Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization, which indicated the court is ready to overturn Roe v. Wade. We're still waiting on the final decision to be released, and with that wait comes uncertainty and fear. But in the meantime, there's still a lot we do know about abortion access nationwide, and what may happen next.

MOBILIZING TO PROTECT COMMUNITIES

On the Frontlines of the Fight Against Mass Deportation

In a new report, the ACLU documented the harms of a federal program that empowers local law enforcement to collaborate with ICE. We spoke with three organizers leading the fight against these law enforcement partnerships about what it's like to live in a community operating under 287(g), how to mobilize communities and empower people to act, and their vision for a future free of this harmful program.

LGBTQ RIGHTS ON THE LINE

The Roe Draft Signals a Potential New Front in the Already-Raging War Against the LGBTQ Community

While the Dobbs v. Jackson Women's Health Organization draft opinion may preview a new front in the war against LGBTQ people, attacks against us – especially youth – have been growing in intensity for several years and have already reached a fever pitch. The fight is upon us, and we need to mobilize now.

PROTECTING RENTERS

New Report Illustrates How Right to Counsel Prevents Evictions and their Discriminatory Impacts on Communities

Our country is facing a historic eviction crisis. Amid a nationwide housing shortage, rents are surging to unprecedented levels, and many renters cannot keep pace. Exacerbating the issue further, renters facing eviction are almost always unrepresented in court proceedings. Our new report illustrates how providing a right to counsel helps keep people in their homes.

PRIVACY AFTER ROE

Impending Threat of Abortion Criminalization Brings New Urgency to the Fight for Digital Privacy

The lack of strong digital privacy protections has profound implications in the face of expanded criminalization of reproductive health care. In light of these authoritarian attacks on bodily autonomy, we must fight with new urgency to ensure that people maintain control over their personal information. If we fail, the repressive surveillance techniques and powers that police and prosecutors have used for decades to wage the racist wars on drugs and terrorism will be marshaled to track, catalogue, and criminalize pregnant people and those seeking basic information about reproductive health issues.

THE RIGHT TO LEARN

How Much Do You Know About Classroom Censorship?

This year, the country has seen a staggering number of policies censoring classroom discussions around topics like race, sex, and gender identity. Coupled with these efforts are bans on books that also center around these subjects – bans which hinder students' ability to learn and talk about these critical topics that impact their daily lives. Test your knowledge about the current wave of classroom censorship measures, as well as some of the books being challenged.
