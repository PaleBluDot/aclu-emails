We want to share the latest victory for immigrants' rights – and tell you how we can all celebrate!

Last Thursday, the Utah Supreme Court approved a new rule to allow eligible DACA (Deferred Action Childhood Arrival) recipients to join the Utah State Bar.

This means DREAMers who have graduated from law school and satisfied every other requirement to practice law in Utah will be able to achieve the professional aspirations they have pursued for years. With this new rule, Utah joins states like New Jersey, California, and Pennsylvania to recognize those incredible contributions of all immigrants.

This change happened thanks to two brave Utah law school graduates who petitioned the court last year to join the Utah Bar. They were supported by the ACLU of Utah, the ACLU Immigrants' Rights Project, and a half-dozen organizations and law firms to bolster their claims. And once the court proposed the new rule, we encouraged elected leaders like Salt Lake City Mayor Erin Mendenhall, activists, and law school professors to submit positive comments.

Although we scored this important victory, we've also experienced setbacks and identified new challenges ahead in our fight for immigrants' rights. We can celebrate and gather our strength together on Thursday, February 13 at the annual fundraiser for the Enriching Utah Coalition (EUC).
