The 2020 Primary Election is happening this month.

But did you know that only 12 of the 29 counties in Utah provide pre-paid postage for mail-in ballots?

This means hundreds of thousands of Utahns – including residents of Cache, Tooele, Washington and Weber counties – will receive ballots indicating they must use a stamp to mail it back.

We believe this is an unfair barrier to voting.

No one should be denied the right to vote because they lack a stamp, are unwilling to leave their house during a pandemic to purchase postage, or are unable to drive to the nearest ballot drop-box.

Fortunately, there is a better way.

We call it the No Stamp, No Problem Campaign.

If you live in a county that does not pre-pay postage for ballots, the U.S. Postal Service will still deliver all unstamped ballots to county clerks and charge the county for the missing postage.

Sound too good to be true? It's not.

The Office of the Lieutenant Governor of Utah, which runs elections in the state, confirms the "No Stamp, No Problem" pledge on their election website. The Lt. Governor's office declares: "Another option is to place your ballot in the mail without postage, and the Postal Service will still deliver your ballot to your county clerk's office."

This campaign is especially important if you live in the following 17 counties that do not provide pre-paid postage:

Beaver, Box Elder, Cache, Carbon, Duchesne, Iron, Millard, Piute, Rich, Sanpete, Sevier, Summit, Tooele, Uintah, Wasatch, Washington, and Weber.
