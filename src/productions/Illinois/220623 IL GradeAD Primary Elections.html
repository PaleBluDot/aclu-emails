The 2022 Illinois primary election is happening next week, on Tuesday, June 28. Voting is your power in deciding the type of future you want to build, the kind of community that we want for everyone.

If you have not yet voted:

  • You can still vote early or return your mail-in ballot on or before June 28.
  • You can register to vote in person in Illinois up until the polls close at 7 p.m. on election day.
  • You can still choose to vote in person if you have a mail-in ballot; just bring it with you if you have it.
  • If you still have a mail-in ballot, deliver it to a drop box, or in person at your polling location or the office of your election authority. If you do need to mail it, make sure it's postmarked before June 28 (not just in a mailbox).
  • Know your rights when you go to the polls. If you experience any problems, call the voter protection hotline at 1-866-OUR-VOTE (find more numbers).
  • And remember – if you are in line when the polls close, stay in line, and you will be able to vote.

There is more information and important dates for this election on our voting resource page, as well as resources and information about how to register and vote in Illinois on our website.
