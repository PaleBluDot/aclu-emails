Right now, the Montana legislature is considering a bill that would make a huge impact on low-income people with suspended licenses. If enough of us speak up, we can pressure the legislature to pass the bill.

Each year, almost 10,000 Montanans lose their driver's licenses because they can't pay their court fees, which has nothing to do with their driving records or public safety. Driver's licenses should only be suspended to keep people safe – suspensions shouldn't be used to punish people because they are poor.

We're demanding that the Montana legislature end debt-based driver's license suspension and stop criminalizing poverty – please sign the petition to your state representative now.

Take Action

Without adequate public transportation, driving is absolutely essential for Montanans to get to work, access child care and healthcare, and conduct basic day-to-day life.

Suspending licenses for inability to pay court debt traps Montanans in a vicious cycle of poverty, debt, and incarceration. It keeps people entangled in the criminal justice system. And not being able to get to work easily makes it harder to pay off the debt.

This is a cruel, counterproductive policy that disproportionately impacts low-income people in our state, and we demand better.

If our state legislators pass the bill, Montanans – no matter which city they live in or how much money they make – will have a far easier time accessing the basic necessities of life.
