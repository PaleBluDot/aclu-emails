Election Day is Tuesday, November 8! Have you voted or are you all set to vote? Our Election Protection Hotline is open until the end of Election Day. We are here to ensure you can exercise your right to vote. Our Hotline staff can help resolve concerns, answer questions, and investigate violations of your rights.

We've pulled together answers to your frequently asked and most pressing questions about voting. Check it out!

Double-check your voting plan:

✅. I mailed in my ballot, have a plan to mail my ballot, OR made a plan to vote in person.

✅. I'm registered to vote or I will register at my polling place on Election Day.

✅. I looked up where my polling place is (because it may have changed.)

✅. I will bring an ID (which is not required for most voters, but safe to bring if you have one) and my phone (if I have one) in case I have to call the ACLU's Hotline.

✅. I researched who and what is on the ballot.

✅. I saved the ACLU-MD Election Protection Hotline Number, 667-219-2625, in case I have problems voting.

✅. I shared this checklist with five friends.

Remember, if you are formerly incarcerated, on probation, or on parole, you do have the right to vote! Voters in pre-trial detention or incarcerated for a misdemeanor also have the right to vote.

Together, let's make sure everyone has a chance to be an empowered voter for the 2022 election. You have the right to a safe voting experience. Call the Election Protection Hotline at 667-219-2625 if you have any concerns.

Thanks for spreading the word about voting and our hotline,
