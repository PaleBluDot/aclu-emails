We had a big win for student fairness this week. Plaintiffs have dismissed their own lawsuit that had sought to overturn the Frederick County School Board’s policies aimed at preventing discrimination, harassment, and stigmatization of transgender and gender non-conforming students, leaving the good policies in place.

James, a transgender student activist in Frederick, filed a motion to intervene in the case to protect the policies, with the support from the national ACLU, Free State Justice and the ACLU of Maryland.

Watch our video to learn more and share it using the hashtag #IAmFrederick.

And there's a big court hearing this week. This Thursday in Baltimore will be the first court hearing in the ACLU’s lawsuit challenging the Trump Administration’s ban on service members who are transgender. We will seek a preliminary injunction that would immediately halt implementation of all aspects of the ban.

Our lead plaintiff, Brock Stone, who is stationed in Maryland, will be there along with other supporters. You can thank Brock for his service to our country on Veteran’s Day on social media, using the hashtag #ThankyouBrock.
