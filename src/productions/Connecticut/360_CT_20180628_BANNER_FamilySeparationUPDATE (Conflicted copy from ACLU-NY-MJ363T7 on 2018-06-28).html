The stories seem too horrific to be true. Immigrant children, some less than a year old, snatched from their parent's arms. But it's all true. Our hearts break every time some new, terrible detail is revealed — tent cities, chain-link cages, kids begging for their parents in tears.

That's why immigrants' rights groups in Connecticut are holding a Free Our Future rally to demand an end to detaining immigrant families in jails and to stand up for immigrants' rights in Connecticut. This country is angry about an unprecedented, Trump-created crisis at our Southern border. We're not fooled by Trump's executive order — the Trump administration is holding thousands of children hostage to get his wish list of anti-immigrant policies, like building a wall, ending protections for children, constructing massive family jails, and deporting as many people as possible.

Free Our Future Rally

Connecticut must do more to protect immigrants here. Cities like Hartford must pass city ordinances to ban ICE and DHS from using local police surveillance to tear families apart in our own towns and cities. This fight isn't over, and we need to do everything we can to make sure Trump doesn't get away with this.

Recent days have shown the power of our voices when we come together and stand up for immigrant children and families. Let that be the fuel that drives us to continue this fight any way we can. Even if you can't show up on July 2, there's still more you can do — whether it's telling your city to pass an ordinance to protect immigrants, telling your state legislators to prioritize immigrants' rights in the next legislative session, or pressuring Congress to put the administration's brutality in check.

There's a lot left to do, but make no mistake: We will win. We only need to keep it up.

See you in the streets,

David McGuire
Executive Director
