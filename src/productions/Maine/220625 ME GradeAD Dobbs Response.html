On Friday, the Supreme Court issued a shameful decision overturning Roe v. Wade. This ruling is an unprecedented attack on women and people who can become pregnant. It will allow extremists across the country to ban abortion and force women and others who can become pregnant into a second-class status by denying them control over their bodies and their futures.

Abortion remains safe and legal in Maine. Because of advocacy the ACLU of Maine has done in partnership with abortion providers, people who have had abortions, and other advocates, Maine has expanded abortion care in recent years. Under state law, MaineCare must pay for abortion care, so low-income people can access abortion. Maine also allows advanced practice clinicians, not just doctors, to provide abortions – expanding access and care.

But we can't be complacent. These attacks won't stop with abortion; the same politicians seeking to control the bodies of pregnant people are coming for our right to access birth control and gender-affirming care, to marry whom we love, and even to vote. We have the power to protect our rights by electing people who are ready to fight with us for our freedom to make our own decisions about our bodies and futures. This November, we must vote like our rights depend on it, because they do.

Like many of you, we are gutted and angry over the Supreme Court's actions this past week. We are especially grateful during these hard times for your partnership in pushing back and moving forward.

We will never stop fighting with you.
