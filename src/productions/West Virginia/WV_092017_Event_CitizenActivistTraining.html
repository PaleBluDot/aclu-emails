Thurs., Oct. 12: ACLU of WV will be in Bluefield for the seventh installment in our Citizen Activist Training program.
  

We hope you can join us on Thursday, October 12 at the Bluefield Parks and Recreation After-School and Day Camp Program for the seventh installment of our Citizen Activist Training program. As part of this ongoing program, we've been travelling the state helping people become more effective activists. Each training covers a different civil liberties issue and advocacy skill.

Next week, we will be discussing how to safely and effectively confront bigotry. We've seen a marked rise in acts of hate over the past 18 months. What can you do as an individual to intervene? What can we do as a society to protect targeted populations and heal communities?

Citizen Activist Training: Confronting Bigotry
Thursday, October 12, 6 - 7 p.m.
Bluefield Recreation Department
1780 Stadium Drive
Bluefield, WV 24701
RSVP on Facebook or by sending an email to Mollie Kennedy.

We will explore the growing governmental and individual attacks on marginalized communities and how we can take meaningful steps to make West Virginia a welcoming home for all people regardless of their group classifications.

We hope to see you there!
