Over the past year, the Cedar Rapids school district has been considering crucial changes to its School Resource Officer (SRO) program, which places armed police officers in all of its high schools and two of its six middle schools (the two with the highest percentage of Black students).

On September 27, instead of listening to research, students, and community concerns, the Cedar Rapids School Board took a step backward and actually voted unanimously to expand the footprint of the SRO program. Now the two middle schools SROs will interact with every middle school and also every elementary school, creating direct armed police contact with children as young as five.

This is so harmful for so many reasons. Other Iowa schools are moving away from police in schools because they don't make students and staff safer. Instead, they disproportionately arrest Black and Brown students, creating a school-to-prison pipeline.

As early as Tuesday, October 12, the Cedar Rapids City Council will vote on the school district's proposed SRO contract. The only true solution is to no longer have SROs in Cedar Rapids schools.

Tell Cedar Rapids City Council members you want them to end the district's SRO program.
