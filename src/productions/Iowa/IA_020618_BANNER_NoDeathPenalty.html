Dear ##first_name[Supporter]##,

In 1968, Iowa wisely repealed the death penalty. Now, a half century later, some legislators want to drag us back to those dark days.

Urge your state senator and respresentative to vote against the death penalty.

Capital punishment was brutal, expensive, and did nothing to prevent violent tragedies. It also sentenced many innocent people to death.

In fact, since 1973, 140 people on death row have been exonerated in the U.S. Those most likely to be wrongly convicted tend to be disproportionately people of color, low-income, or those with a mental disability.

Read the story of the Texas man who wrongly served 18 years on death row, and who now is an employee of the ACLU of Texas.

We need to focus on criminal justice solutions that are effective and fair. The death penalty is neither.

Tell your representatives that Iowans need effective criminal justice reforms, not outdated ones.

Sincerely,


Mark Stringer
Executive Director
ACLU of Iowa
