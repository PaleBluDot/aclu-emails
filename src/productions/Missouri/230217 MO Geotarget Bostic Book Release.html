I'm pleased to share that on Monday, February 27, the St. Louis County Library's 2023 Black History Celebration will host our former client Bobby Bostic, author of "Time: Endless Moments in Prison." Bobby will be joined in conversation with Pulitzer Prize-winning St. Louis Post-Dispatch columnist Tony Messenger.

Bobby Bostic,
"Time: Endless Moments in Prison"

Monday, February 27, 7 p.m.
Door open at 6 p.m. St. Louis County Library - Florissant Valley Branch
195 New Florissant Rd. S.
Florissant, MO 63031


At the age of 16, Bobby Bostic was sentenced to 241 years behind bars for armed robbery. In 2010, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled that it is unconstitutional to sentence a juvenile to life without the possibility of parole for non-homicidal crimes. Through the efforts of the ACLU and the now-retired judge who originally sentenced him, Bobby Bostic finally left prison as a free man in 2022. While incarcerated, Bobby earned his GED and wrote several books. He will discuss his memoir "Time: Endless Moments in Prison" with St. Louis Post Dispatch columnist Tony Messenger.

Books will be available for purchase and signing at the event. I hope to see you there.
