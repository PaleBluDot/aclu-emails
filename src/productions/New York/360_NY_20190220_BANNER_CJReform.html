Dear ##SM_RULE_360 Email Salutation##,

Everyone accused of a crime is supposed to be innocent until proven guilty. But in New York, because of our laws, tens of thousands of New Yorkers every year are denied this basic right.

TAKE ACTION: Demand real criminal justice reform NOW

State lawmakers have a historic opportunity to fix our state’s bail, speedy trial, and discovery laws after last year’s elections put progressive leadership in charge of the State Senate.

In New York, being charged with a crime often means having only two options: pleading guilty to a crime or staying locked up in jail. People who have not been convicted of a crime often sit in jail just because they can’t afford to pay their way out.

Then, our state’s speedy trial law allows cases to drag out. The constitution guarantees a right to a speedy trial, but waiting for trial in New York—even for a low-level offense—can take years.

And when it comes time to fight their case in court, New Yorkers don’t get a fair chance. Prosecutors can keep evidence secret, which means that defendants are completely in the dark when making decisions about whether to forfeit their rights to trial. No one should plead guilty without knowing why they’re accused of a crime, but that is what is happening right now because of New York’s flawed discovery law.

Tell lawmakers to fight for fairness in the courts. We need real criminal justice reform.

Thank you for standing up for a more just New York,

New York Civil Liberties Union
