County Executive Laura Curran did the right thing. Then she caved.

Last week Curran told Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) that they couldn't run their business out of the county jail.

That was the right move. Our law enforcement and other officials should be in the business of working for everyone in our community – not bending to the cruel deportation desires of President Trump by working for ICE. When police work for ICE, it only breaks down trust with immigrant New Yorkers, leaving everyone worse off.

But then this week, under pressure, Curran buckled and said ICE could just set up shop nearby – this time on the grounds of Nassau University Medical Center.

She replaced one horrible idea with another.

Not only is Curran laying out the welcome mat for ICE, but now immigrant residents will also have to fear a trip to the hospital. That's why we need you to take action right now.

Take Action

Everyone should be able to turn to police or medical personnel when they need help, without fear for themselves or their family. County leaders need to draw clear lines between those who protect and serve our community and Washington's cruel anti-immigrant agenda.

Executive Curran's first decision was the right one. We need to tell her to ICE to pack up and that Nassau County works for everyone.

Tell County Executive Laura Curran: Our county facilities are not home to Trump's deportation agenda.

Thank you for taking action and standing with immigrant New Yorkers,
