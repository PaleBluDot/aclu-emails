For many years, Idaho's public defense system has been broken. Individuals in the criminal justice system have seen firsthand how a lack of financial resources and overworked public defenders have resulted in a loss of adequate representation in courtrooms across the state.

Visit our website to learn how you can help improve Idaho’s criminal justice system.

But now comes an opportunity for those directly impacted by our failing system to help shape the future of public defense in Idaho.

The Idaho Public Defense Commission is currently drafting rules that will guide how they do work in several areas, including standards that public defenders would be expected to meet. Review our 2017 Public Defense Commission Rules guide to learn more.

Before these rules go for approval, the Commission is accepting feedback from the public. In order to create rules that improve Idaho's public defense system, they need to hear from those most impacted by our current broken system – individuals who have been unable to receive the representation they need by a public defender in their criminal case.

You have the power to fix Idaho's public defense system and we encourage you to share your story with the Commission. The deadline to submit feedback is Wednesday, October 25. You can share your feedback in a number of ways.

  1. Write the Commission at: Idaho Public Defense Commission 816 W. Bannock Street, Suite 201 Boise, ID 83702 2.
  2. Email the Commission at: info@pdc.idaho.gov
  3. Submit comments online at: www.pdc.idaho.gov. Visit the page titled "Proposed and Pending Rules" and use the online forms.
  4. Share your story in person at an upcoming public hearing:
