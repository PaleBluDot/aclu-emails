While the ACLU of Idaho was officially established in 1993, Idaho had already played a role in shaping civil liberties across the country. Reed v. Reed, a pivotal case for women's rights, began in Boise and made it all the way to the U.S. Supreme Court.

In 1971, Ruth Bader Ginsburg, then a volunteer attorney with the ACLU, co-authored a brief with ACLU Director Mel Wulf on behalf of Sally Reed, who challenged the Idaho law that gave preference to men for appointment as the administrator of a deceased person's estate. The U.S. Supreme Court unanimously decided that discrimination based on gender was unconstitutional because it denies equal protection under the 14th Amendment.

As we reflect on the fight for civil rights and civil liberties in Idaho, we honor all the key figures who have played a role.
