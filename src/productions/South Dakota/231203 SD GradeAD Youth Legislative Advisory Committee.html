Ever find yourself wondering if what you're doing is enough? Do you want to see South Dakota change for the better? Keep reading.

When we show up and rally behind our community, great things can happen. Don't forget how powerful your voice is as a constituent in South Dakota!

If you're ready to get more involved, I've got a great opportunity for you on the ACLU's Youth Legislative Advisory Committee! Members of this committee will gain a better understanding of South Dakota politics and how anyone can affect the process. Plus, we'll have a lot of fun getting to know advocates from across the state along the way.

More about the committee: The ACLU of South Dakota's Youth Legislative Advisory Committee meets virtually every week to discuss proposed legislation, local politics, and how specific current issues affect our lives and our communities.

Meetings last about an hour, during which we'll share news about proposed bills, policy analysis, and ACLU messaging guidance. You'll then have the opportunity to provide feedback, discuss legislative strategy and committee testimony and what steps we can take together next.

For youth who are 26 years old and younger and interested in learning more about how the legislature makes laws and how the ACLU works within that system to influence the outcome, participating on the advisory committee is a great start and a great way to gain experience for future opportunities.
