Last month, we asked all 36 of Oregon's elected district attorneys (DAs) to publicly share their views on important issues in the criminal justice system. For instance, what are their stances on jailing material witnesses, non-unanimous jury convictions, and how young people are treated under Measure 11?

How does your elected district attorney stand on key issues?

Unfortunately, DAs are refusing to share their positions. We were surprised, because DAs are elected officials that answer to the voters, so shouldn't they be more transparent with the public about their policy positions? You have a right to know which policies they support. You are their constituent, and you care about the criminal justice system.

Take action and ask your DA to share their policy positions.

District attorneys, like any other elected official, should be open about their positions on crucial issues. We launched They Report to You to help mobilize grassroots power in Oregon around these powerful players in our local criminal justice system.

Transparency around these important questions is a crucial first step toward building a justice system that is fair, safe, and effective. Please take action today.
