Indigenous Justice means the freedom and ability of Indigenous people to exercise our rights and sovereignty over ourselves, our people, and our land. As we look back on another Indigenous Peoples' Day and look ahead to Native American Heritage Month, it is an honor to share a significant victory for Indigenous Justice in our state.

After nearly two years of litigation, a Lakota family we have been representing has settled their civil rights lawsuit against a Nebraska school district.

Alice and Norma's case argued that a school official had violated their rights and religious beliefs by cutting the hair of their children without parental permission. The case drew national attention, given how it called to mind the history of forced assimilation in boarding schools.

Thanks to our clients' courage and persistence, Cody-Kilgore Unified School District agreed to a consent decree that will stop employees from cutting students' hair, provide cultural sensitivity training, and ensure recognition of Native American Heritage Month and Indigenous Peoples' Day.

The consent decree represents a significant step toward Indigenous Justice in our state.

And while we celebrate this victory alongside our clients, we also acknowledge the work continues, not just on Indigenous Peoples Day or Native American Heritage Month, but every day.
