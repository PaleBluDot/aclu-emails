We are just a handful of days into the new year. As we put tires to the pavement on the road ahead of us, I find myself energized when I remember just how much we have recently accomplished together.

In 2022, ACLU supporters like you helped stave off extreme threats to abortion access in the Legislature. Together, we stopped a bill that would have censored conversations about race and gender in our schools. We spoke up for LGBTQ+ equality. And we showed up at the ballot box with a vote to advance economic justice by increasing Nebraska's minimum wage.

As we head into a new year and a new legislative session, it is critical to remember these victories because we are sure to see new challenges.

State senators have promised new extreme abortion bans and bills that will target LGBTQ+ youth. We will likely see new threats to students' right to learn and to our fundamental right to vote – including proposed rules for how the state implements voter ID. And there is no doubt we will see more “tough on crime” policies that only result in more people in prison and more wasted taxpayer dollars instead of improved community safety.

Our team is ready for whatever 2023 will bring. And I hope you are too. Together, we will keep building power as we remind officials that "We the People" means all of us.

Here's to another year of bold advocacy. Make sure you are following us on Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, or TikTok. And please resolve to share our emails and posts with friends as we start a new year and face new challenges.

Thanks for being with us,

Mindy Rush Chipman
