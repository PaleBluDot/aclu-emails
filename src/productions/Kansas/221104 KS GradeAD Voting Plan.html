Know your voting rights

{include "default_salutation"} –

On Tuesday, your rights are once again on the ballot. Here are some quick tips to be sure you're ready to make a difference in the voting booth.

  1. Vote early or on Election Day.

    All Kansans can currently vote early at any polling location in their county; early voting ends at noon on Monday.

    If you plan to vote on the day of the election (November 8), make sure you know your polling location. You can use your address to find it.

    If you have already received a mail-in ballot, remember all ballots MUST be postmarked on or before Election Day and received in the county election office by the close of business on the Friday following the election.

  2. Have your ID ready.

    In Kansas, you are required to show a photo ID when you vote. If you're voting by mail, you must either include your driver's license number or a copy of your ID.

    Find a list of accepted forms of IDs.

  3. Call the Election Protection hotline with any issues.

    Our Election Protection team is already fielding calls for this election.

    If you have problems voting or have additional questions, please call the national, non-partisan Election Protection Hotline:

    • English: 1-866-OUR-VOTE (1-866-687-8683)
    • Spanish: 1-888-VE-Y-VOTA (1-888-839-8682)
    • Arabic: 1-844-YALLA-US (1-844-925-5287)
    • Bengali, Cantonese, Hindi, Urdu, Korean, Mandarin, Tagalog, or Vietnamese: 1-888-274-8683

    Please Note: ACLU of Kansas staff, board, and volunteers are not election officials, nor do they hold themselves out as such.

  4. Make sure your friends and family are ready to vote, too.

It's important for us to take care of each other, and that includes accountability with our communities. After you have a voting plan to make your voice heard, check in with others and share our Know Your Voting Rights resource on social media to help them make their plan.
