Vermont's Fair and Impartial Policing policy is in danger.

In September, the Vermont Criminal Justice Training Council, as directed by the Vermont legislature, adopted a Fair and Impartial Policing Policy (FIPP) to protect Vermonters from biased policing statewide. The FIPP was written with strong input from the Human Rights Commission, the ACLU, and community organizations such as Migrant Justice and Justice for All, and was determined to be fully compliant with immigration law by the Vermont Attorney General's Office.

It's time to take action for racial justice in Vermont. Sign the petition and add your name to Change.org.

The version the Council is proposing NOW severely compromises the tenets of transparency and accountability. It also departs from previously agreed language limiting local law enforcement's role in immigration enforcement. These changes endanger victims and witnesses of crime, migrant dairy workers, and the thousands of other non-citizen Vermonters that attend our schools, work in our communities, and keep our economy vibrant.

The FIPP is an important tool in the fight for racial justice in our state, but only if it includes measures for real accountability and real protections for people who are most likely to be targeted by law enforcement.

The original Fair and Impartial Policing Policy ensures that all of us receive equal treatment under the law, regardless of race, ethnicity, immigration status, national origin, religion, gender or any other aspect of who we are or how we're perceived by law enforcement. The Council meets tomorrow behind closed doors.

Please help protect FIPP today by signing the petition to strengthen protections from biased policing.
