Now is the time for bold action to advance racial justice and reimagine the role of police in our communities. In response to the nationwide movement for police accountability, state lawmakers have introduced some modest reforms, but they are unlikely to change the status quo or result in the transformational changes Vermonters are demanding.

That's why the ACLU and a diverse group of organizations are introducing a ten-part action plan for police reform in Vermont.

READ OUR PLAN

We are calling on lawmakers to limit the unchecked power of police and make much-needed investments in Vermont communities — and we need your help to amplify that call by contacting your public officials and asking them to invest in communities, not policing.

This August, the Vermont legislature will host a series of three “virtual meetings” to obtain community input on police reform:

  • Tomorrow, Thursday, August 6, 1 p.m.
  • Wednesday, August 12, 6 p.m.
  • Sunday, August 16, 4 p.m.

These meetings are critical opportunities to make our voices heard, and we need you to (virtually) attend, or to complete a short survey telling your representatives to prioritize our plan for transforming policing in Vermont.

To sign up to give testimony or fill out the survey for legislators, visit our web page.

It's time to insist that our laws and investments match our values as Vermonters and affirm – now and always – that Black Lives Matter. Please join us in asking our public officials to prioritize people and communities, not policing and prisons.
