As the premier civil rights and liberties organization in Nevada, we file many public records requests, and it’s clear the Nevada Public Records Act is not living up to its promise of open government. Some agencies ignore or obstruct requests with impunity, and some charge excessive fees that make public records unobtainable for many Nevadans and nonprofits.

That’s why we joined the Right to Know Nevada coalition. It’s one of the broadest coalitions we’ve ever seen, consisting of nonprofit and advocacy groups across the political spectrum as well as journalists, researchers, and community members.

The coalition is working to update and modernize our public records law to require accessibility, cooperation, and accountability from Nevada agencies. These records belong to the public. We hope you’ll join us in standing up for your right to know.

Thanks for sticking with us,

ACLU of Nevada
