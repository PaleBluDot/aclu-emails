Voting is the cornerstone of our democracy. Unfortunately, Mississippi's voting system is outdated, wasteful, and inefficient. It's time to bring Mississippi into the 21st century of voting. Learn more about voting reforms on Friday, December 8 at Be Counted: A Public Forum About Expanding Voter Access.

Common-sense voting reforms like no-excuse early voting and online voter registration help to improve voter access, boost voter turnout, make the voting process easier, and save taxpayer dollars.

This forum will feature panelists from the Mississippi State Legislature, Mississippi NAACP, and the Secretary of State's office. This event is a partnership effort between the ACLU of Mississippi, League of Women Voters-Jackson, Mississippi Votes, and Women for Progress of Mississippi.

Lunch will be served, and registration is required. Please RSVP by sending an email to office@aclu-ms.org or on Facebook.

Let's work together to modernize Mississippi's voting process to Let People Vote!

Sincerely,

ACLU of Mississippi
