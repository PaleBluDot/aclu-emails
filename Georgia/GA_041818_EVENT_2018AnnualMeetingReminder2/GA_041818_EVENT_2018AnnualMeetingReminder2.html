ACLU of Georgia Annual Membership Meeting & Reception

Don't miss your opportunity to attend the ACLU of Georgia's Annual Membership Meeting & Reception on Thursday, April 26 in Atlanta!

We have fewer than 100 available spaces remaining, so RSVP today.

The meeting will feature special guest speaker, Dale Ho, Director of the National Voting Rights Project. Dale has active cases in over dozen states and is an adjunct professor of law at NYU Law School. He is a frequent commentator on voting rights issues, appearing on several MSNBC television shows, has written opinion pieces for the New York Times, and is widely published on redistricting and voting rights in law reviews, including Harvard Civil Rights-Civil Liberties Law Review.

With the support of our members, our work together is paying off! We plan to highlight our many 2017 successes and provide an update on 2018 strategy. In our fight for voter rights, we put the Hall County Board of Elections on notice, notifying the board that its proposal to eliminate bilingual materials for its Spanish speaking U.S. citizens may violate the U.S. Voting Rights Act of 1965. The ACLU of Georgia caused the secretary of state to reach a settlement to keep 160,000 voters on the active voter rolls as required by law and update the addresses of 35,000 registered voters who had recently moved within the same county as required by state law. We also demanded that the Secretary of State change its misleading voter registration forms.

The reception begins at 6:00 p.m., and the meeting begins at 7:15 p.m. Enjoy complimentary hors d'oeuvres and beverages. Admission is free, but an RSVP is required. Parking is available onsite. Lyft is highly encouraged.

Thank you to our event sponsors!
