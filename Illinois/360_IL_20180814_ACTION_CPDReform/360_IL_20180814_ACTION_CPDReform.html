Dear ACLU Supporter,

The system for policing in Chicago is broken and needs repair. Your voice is needed for real reform of the Chicago Police Department.

The Illinois Attorney General and the City of Chicago recently unveiled a draft agreement to guide reform of the CPD. The plan, a “consent decree” to be submitted to a federal court for final approval, aims to address problems detailed in a scathing report from the U.S. Department of Justice last year. The draft agreement lacks essential reforms.

The ACLU, alongside our clients and community partners, recently released our official response on how to improve the draft plan: A Commitment to Real Reform. You can add your voice to demand real reform now.

Tell the attorney general to strengthen the agreement so that CPD is fully transparent and accountable to all Chicago neighborhoods.

Help ensure these important improvements are included in the final version of the consent decree. The Illinois Attorney General is accepting public comments — but the time is running out. The period for comments closes on this Friday, August 17.

Act quickly — add your voice in calling for real reform.

Together we can help bring real reform to the Chicago Police Department and make it accountable and better able to serve every neighborhood in Chicago.

Sincerely,

Kathy Hunt Muse
ACLU of Illinois
