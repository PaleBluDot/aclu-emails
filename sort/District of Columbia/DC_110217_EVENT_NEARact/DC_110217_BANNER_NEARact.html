Passed unanimously by the D.C. Council in 2016, the NEAR (Neighborhood Engagement Achieves Results) Act establishes a set of long-term programs and policies to address public safety in D.C. These programs and policies have been empirically proven to reduce shootings and violence in cities around the US.

With full implementation, the NEAR Act would begin to de-emphasize the use of policing and force as the primary tactic for ensuring neighborhood safety in D.C. It would protect vulnerable communities and residents in high-crime areas of our city from further harm. By focusing on deterring crime and providing programs and services to these communities, people are reached before they enter the criminal justice system. The policy will pave the way for the long-overdue transformation of these vulnerable populations and neighborhoods towards a deeper culture of safety, support, and opportunity.

We need your help:

Despite being fully-funded for 2017, most provisions in the NEAR Act have not yet been implemented.

For the NEAR Act to reach its full potential, we need D.C. residents like yourself to make sure our elected officials fully and faithfully implement all of the comprehensive approaches in the law. To do so, we are training engaged D.C. residents to be NEAR Act Ambassadors to show up to D.C. Council hearings, community events, ANC meetings, candidate fundraisers, and campaign events. Together, we will hold D.C. councilmembers to their promise of protecting the most vulnerable neighborhoods in our city from further violence with a better approach.

We hope you'll join us on Tuesday, November 14 for this important event, and spread the word.
