Dear ##first_name[Supporter]##,

Imagine being called a terrorist by your peers, every day. Imagine being afraid to go to the bathroom. Imagine those things happening, and you are 8-years-old.

Last week, the ACLU of Maine released a report called We Belong Here: Eliminating Inequity in Education for Immigrants and Students of Color in Maine.

The report describes the bullying and harassment that kids of color face in Maine schools. At the same time, it highlights programs that some Maine schools have implemented to improve their school culture and end disproportionate treatment.

Dealing with this isn't a matter of opinion – schools have a legal requirement to address discrimination according to the Constitution.

We've created resources for parents, students, teachers, and principals, and we're going to work with them to strengthen their schools.

Kids have civil rights too, and your support of the ACLU is what allows us to defend them. Thank you for caring and choosing to do something about it.

Alison Beyea, Esq.
Executive Director
ACLU of Maine
