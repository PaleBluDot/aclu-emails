The state of Nebraska is planning the execution of Carey Dean Moore on August 14. This will be the first use of the death penalty in Nebraska in 21 years and the first use nationally of a four-drug combination used for lethal injection.

Tell Governor Ricketts this clandestine execution should not be done in your name.

The Ricketts administration has refused to disclose public records about the purchase and testing of these drugs — as well as the training of the state employees charged with conducting the execution — despite a court order.

The law requires that these records be public as a check on the executive branch to ensure that the death penalty drugs and protocol will not result in a prolonged or unnecessarily painful execution, amounting to cruel and unusual punishment.

Register your dissent of a death penalty carried out with protocol shrouded in secrecy.

An administration confident enough to execute a man on behalf of the state should be confident enough in its protocol to release public records about it to Nebraskans. That’s what the law requires.
