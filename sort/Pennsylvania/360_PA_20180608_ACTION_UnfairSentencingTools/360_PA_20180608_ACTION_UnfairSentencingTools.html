Dear ##first_name[Supporter]##,

The Pennsylvania Commission on Sentencing is considering a proposal to create a so-called risk assessment tool that it claims can predict future criminality. The tool would create a risk score that judges would see at sentencing.

Here’s the problem: this tool is rooted in the racial disparities already plaguing Pennsylvania’s criminal justice system.

One of your state legislators is a member of the Pennsylvania Commission on Sentencing, which will vote on implementing the instrument on Wednesday.

Will you contact them right now and urge them to reject the proposed risk assessment tool?

From over-policing in communities of color to expanding the definition of recidivism to ensnare people on minimal violations, the risk assessment tool would only further entrench racial bias in sentencing. Even the commission itself acknowledges that the margin of error for this tool is between 30 and 40 percent.

If this instrument is implemented, it will mean longer sentences for more people, and it will continue to deepen the disproportionate incarceration rates of Black and Latinx Pennsylvanians.

Instead of relying on a broken and biased criminal justice system to assess risk at sentencing, judges should use their discretion and make a sentencing decision based on the individual before them.

Take action today to help stem the racial disparities at sentencing and take a step toward ending mass incarceration in Pennsylvania.
