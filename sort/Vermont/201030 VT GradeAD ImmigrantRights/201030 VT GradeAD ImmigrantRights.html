We're writing with good news! We finalized a settlement agreement in our lawsuit against Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) on Wednesday for unlawfully targeting Migrant Justice and its leaders in coordination with Vermont DMV.

With this settlement, we have held ICE accountable for violating the rights of local activists, and Vermont immigrant communities will be safer as a result.

The ACLU and our allies filed this case in November 2018, following years of harassment of immigrants' rights leaders in Vermont by federal immigration agents acting in coordination with Vermont's DMV. As part of a larger pattern of suppressing immigrant activism nationwide, agents infiltrated Migrant Justice through the use of a civilian informant, invasively surveilled its members, and mined their social media pages for information, leading to arrests, detentions, and deportations of its members.

This campaign of repression was conducted in direct retaliation for Migrant Justice's successful activism.

Our clients had already settled their claims against DMV, sharply limiting DMV's involvement with ICE. In Wednesday's settlement, ICE has agreed not to deport our clients who were targeted with retaliatory arrests, to instruct agents not to target people for exercising First Amendment rights, and to pay the plaintiffs a monetary award.

These settlements send an important message that Vermont will not tolerate attacks on immigrant communities.

For the past four years, our nationwide efforts have succeeded in stopping much of the Trump administration's anti-immigrant agenda in its tracks, and although the fight is not over, this lawsuit is another victory for immigrants' rights in Vermont and across the country.

Thank you for helping us to defend the rights and dignity of our immigrant friends, neighbors, and family members, and for making these critical victories possible.
