Oregon voters overwhelmingly defeated Measures 105 and 106! With our ballots, we affirmed that regardless of what is happening within the national political landscape, we can still make progress in Oregon when we organize together.

I am so inspired by the level of engagement from ACLU supporters, and by the diverse and dynamic coalitions that came together to defeat these ballot measures. This election cycle, ACLU volunteers in Oregon sent thousands of postcards to voters, made over 50,000 phone calls, and knocked on over 20,000 doors to talk with their neighbors about these important issues.

Together, we reaffirmed what the state legislature said over 30 years ago: No one should be targeted based on the color of their skin, their accent, or their perceived immigration status. Oregon voters rejected racism, xenophobia, and the politics of division and fear-mongering.

We also showed once again that we strongly believe that everyone should have access to the full range of reproductive health care, including abortion, no matter how much money they have or what kind of insurance they carry.

These are tremendous victories and we should celebrate them well. But make no mistake, our work isn’t done.

In the 2019 legislative session, we can continue to make progress on immigrant rights, juvenile justice reform, privacy protections, and more! Save the date to join us for an ACLU of Oregon Lobby Day on Presidents Day, February 18, 2019 at the state capital in Salem.
