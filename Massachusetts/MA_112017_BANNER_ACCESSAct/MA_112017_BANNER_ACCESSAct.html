Today, Governor Baker signed the ACCESS Act, ensuring that Massachusetts residents can obtain birth control without having to pay a dime out of pocket – despite the Trump administration's policies rolling back federal protections for this basic preventative healthcare.

With your support, we fought for – and won! – the first legislation passed anywhere in the country in response to Trump's blistering attack on women and reproductive rights.

The ACCESS Act reasserts Massachusetts' position as a national leader in reproductive rights and health care. It represents a big step forward, yet this administration, under the guise of religious freedom, continues to try to limit our access to many of our basic rights.

There is no religious or moral standing for denying preventative healthcare to millions of people. Decisions about family planning should be between a patient and their doctor. That's why we will steadfastly continue to fight against these restrictive policies – in the State House, in the courts, and in the community.
