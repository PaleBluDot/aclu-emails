Dear ##SM_RULE_360 Email Salutation##,

We're ending the year with great news for immigrants in New England, and we wanted to make sure you heard about it.

Thanks to a major legal victory, hundreds – if not thousands – of people caught up in Trump's deportation machine will now have a chance to be with their families while their cases proceed.

Earlier this year, we shared the story of Gilberto, our client who was snatched by ICE in front of his wife and children. At his bond hearing, the government had to prove nothing, while Gilberto had to prove a negative – that he shouldn't be in jail while he fights deportation. He spent three months separated from his family.

After securing Gilberto's release, we filed a class action lawsuit, arguing that forcing immigrants to bear the burden of proof in this way is unconstitutional. In November, a federal judge agreed, ordering the government to provide fair bond hearings for current and future detainees in New England.

This is a monumental victory. As of Friday, December 13, immigrants in New England will no longer be jailed by default or forced to pay exorbitant fees to secure their release.

As we reflect on a year of struggles and hard-fought victories, we're more thankful than ever for your support, and we look forward with hope to the next stage of the fight.

In solidarity,

ACLU of Massachusetts
