While North Carolina’s COVID-19 curve is flattening the public health criss is ramping up in our prisons, which are set to become the epicenter of North Carolina's fight against the virus. Public health experts are universal in their view that the only way to stemm the tide of illness an death in crowded prisons is by substantially decreasing the number of incarcerated people so that social distancing is possible.

To protect the public health and safety of thousands of incarcerated people and the staff that work in these prisons, as well as the health of the broader North Carolina public, the Governor must reduce the number of medically vulnerable people in prison, and reduce the overall prison population to allow for social distancing.
