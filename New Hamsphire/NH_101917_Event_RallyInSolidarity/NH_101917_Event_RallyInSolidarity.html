Dear ##first_name[Supporter]##,

This Friday, October 20 at the Moakley Courthouse in Boston, a federal court will consider a plea by the Indonesian Christian community to postpone the rushed deportations of dozens of their members who have resided peacefully in the U.S., under voluntary ICE supervision, for decades.

There will be a rally in solidarity with our immigrant brothers and sisters who are at risk of deportation – to show support for these valued community members and urge ICE to stop this rush to deportation.

All they are asking for is a chance to explain why they fear being returned to Indonesia and the persecution they would face there as Christians. ICE has now tried to remove them from the country in a matter of weeks. The due process clause of the fifth amendment to the U.S. constitution, international law, and U.S. immigration law requires a process for such an assessment. They have no criminal records, and each have US citizen or DACA children. This rushed deportation would leave deportees vulnerable to persecution and would tear families apart.

This event is meant to be peaceful, not disrupt traffic or entry to the building, and not an anti-Trump event. Please allow extra time for parking.

We're looking forward to seeing you all there in solidarity.

Christina Gibson
Outreach and Communication Coordinator
ACLU of New Hampshire
