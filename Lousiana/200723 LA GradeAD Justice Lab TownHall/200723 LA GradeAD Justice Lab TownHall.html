Just over a month ago, we launched "Justice Lab: Putting Racist Policing on Trial" – an intensive litigation effort to challenge racially discriminatory policing practices and combat police violence against people of color across the state. Through Justice Lab, we seek to create a litigation blueprint for every other state in the country and help end the era of police impunity and state-sanctioned violence once and for all.

The response has been overwhelming – but now we need your help. Justice Lab is actively seeking potential plaintiffs who've recently experienced racist policing. If you've had a negative race-based interaction with state or local police in Louisiana on or after May 1, 2020, we want to hear from you. Contact us at justicelab@laaclu.org or call 504-522-0628 and leave a message.

On August 6, we will be hosting a virtual town hall meeting on Justice Lab for the community to learn more about why the campaign was created, the work we've done so far, and how to get involved – even if you aren't an attorney.
